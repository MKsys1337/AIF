# Dual Licensing

AIF uses a dual-licensing model to balance open-source availability with commercial sustainability.

## Open-Source License: AGPL-3.0-or-later

The default license is the **GNU Affero General Public License v3.0 or later**.

This means:

- You can **use, modify, and distribute** AIF freely
- If you modify AIF and deploy it as part of a **network service** (e.g., a social media platform, a CDN, a SaaS product), you must **publish your modifications** under the same license
- This applies even if users only interact with your service over a network (the "Affero" clause)

The AGPL ensures that improvements to AIF benefit the entire community.

## Commercial License

If the AGPL is not compatible with your use case — for example:

- You want to integrate AIF into **proprietary software**
- Your organization's **legal policy** prohibits AGPL dependencies
- You need to distribute AIF in a **closed-source product**
- You want to offer AIF as part of a **commercial SaaS** without publishing your source code

A **commercial license** is available that removes the AGPL obligations.

### What the commercial license includes

- Use AIF in proprietary products and services
- Distribute AIF as part of closed-source applications
- No requirement to publish your source code
- Priority support and consultation available

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use AIF in my open-source project?**
A: Yes — as long as your project is compatible with the AGPL-3.0.

**Q: Can I use AIF internally without publishing my code?**
A: Internal use within your organization (not exposed over a network to external users) is generally permitted under the AGPL. However, if your service processes images for external users, the Affero clause applies.
